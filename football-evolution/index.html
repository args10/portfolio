Balls in 1800's

  • 1836 Charles Goodyear patented vulcanized rubber
  • 1855 Designed and built the first vulcanized rubber footballs
  • 1862 H.J. Lindon developed first inflatable rubber bladders for balls
  • 1872 Balls were made "spherical with a circumference of 27 to 28 inches"
  • 1888 Mitre and Thomlinson's (Glasgow) started mass production of footballs

First 8-Panel Ball

  • Stronger rubber and could withstand heavier pressure

  • These balls would bounce easier and yet could be kicked

1930 World Cup


Two different balls were used in the final. Argentina supplied the first-half ball ('Tiento') and led 2–1 at the break; hosts Uruguay supplied the second-half ball ('T-Model' which was larger and heavier) and as it turned out won 4–2.

Advent of Floodlights

In 1951 a white ball was first permitted to help spectators see the ball easier with the advent of floodlights. The leather was simply white washed to produce the white ball. Orange balls were also first introduced in the 1950's to help see the ball in the snow.

  • 32 hand-stitched panels: 12 black pentagons, 20 white hexagons

  • Derived name from "Star of Television" better visibility on BW TVs

  • Superior ball control, allowed players curve ball in the air

Goal Line Technology

Method used to determine when the ball has completely crossed the goal line in between the goal-posts and underneath the crossbar with the assistance of electronic devices

  • GoalRef system relies on the principle of electromagnetic induction
  • A low frequency magnetic field is generated around the goal
  • The ball is fitted with a passive electronic circuit
  • Passage of the ball over the line causes change in the goal's magnetic field
  • The system sends an encrypted signal in real time to a wrist watch worn by the referee
  • Developed by Fraunhofer IIS with Select Sport (Denmark)
